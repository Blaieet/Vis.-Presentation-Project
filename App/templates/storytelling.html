The Leicester City F.C. Miracle run

On the 2nd of May of 2016 the Leicester City F.C, also know as "the Foxes", became the Premier League Season 2015/2016 champion. Not Chelsea, or Arsenal, or Manchester United, teams burnished with petro-dollars and star figures. At the start of the season, bookmakers had Leicester at an incredible 5.000 to 1 odds to win the championship. The best position Leicester got in all the Premier League history was second, and that happened in 1929. In fact, in the previous season, they almost did not escape the relegation zone of the league. This is their story, in numbers.

The Leicester City F.C. was founded in 1884, earlier than Chelsea F.C., FC Barcelona or Real Madrid CF. Nevertheless, till 7 years ago, it was not considered a top-ten football club, not even one of the best top twenty England teams. It was in 2014, when after 6 years of poor results in second division, they managed to climb the ladder promote to Premier League, the highest-level soccer league in England.

Chart / Chapter 1

Even though they almost won the play-offs for promotion in the 2011-12 season, the Leicester journey in the England second division, also known as English Football League, is quite mediocre and underwhelming. Therefore, their 2013-14 season run was a total upset for everyone, finishing first in the classification with over 8 points from the second best, the "Burnley F.C."

The hype of the promotion to the Premier League ended quickly for the Foxes as they finished the season in 14th place with 41 points. In fact, they did a first "small" miracle run: they survived from relegation after being at the bottom of the classification at Christmas. It's the only team in the England soccer history that managed to get less than 20 points from 29 matches and not get relegated. What an upturn!
